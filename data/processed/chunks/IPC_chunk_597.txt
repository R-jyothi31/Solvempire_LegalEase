(b) A writes the word “accepted” on a piece of paper and signs it with Z’s 
name, in order that B may afterwards write on the paper a bill of exchange 
drawn by B upon Z, and negotiate the bill as though it had been accepted by Z. 
A is guilty of forgery; and if B, knowing the fact, draws the bill upon the paper 
pursuant to A’s intention, B is also guilty of forgery.
(c) A picks up a bill of exchange payable to the order of a different person of 
the same name. A endorses the bill in his own name, intending to cause it to 
be believed that it was endorsed by the person whose order it was payable; 
here A has committed forgery.
