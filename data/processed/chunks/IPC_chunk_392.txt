Reasonable man’s—Test
The accused, a naval officer, was charged with the murder of P, a businessman
of Bombay, for having illicit intimacy with his wife. On coming to know from 
his wife about the illicit relationship with the deceased, he went to the ship, 
took from the stores a semi-automatic revolver and six cartridges on a false 
pretext, loaded the same, went to the flat of P entered in his bedroom and shot
him dead after a heated exchange of words. The court held that the test to be 
applied is that of the effect of the provocation on a reasonable man; and in 
applying that test it is of particular importance to consider whether a sufficient
interval has elapsed since the receiving of the information which caused the 
provocation to allow a reasonable man to cool down; K.M. Nanavati v. State of 
Maharasthra, AIR 1962 SC 605.
