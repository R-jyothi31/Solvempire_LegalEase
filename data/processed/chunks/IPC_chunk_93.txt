Section 108. Abettor
A person abets an offence, who abets either the commission of an offence, or 
the commission of an act which would be an offence, if committed by a person 
capable of law of committing an offence with the same intention or knowledge 
as that of the abettor.
Explanation 1
The abetment of the illegal omission of an act may amount to an offence 
although the abettor may not himself be bound to do that act.
Explanation 2
To constitute the offence of abetment it is not necessary that the act abetted 
should be committed, or that the effect requisite to constitute the offence 
should be caused.
Illustrations
(a) A instigates B to murder C. B refuses to do so. A is guilty of abetting B to 
commit murder.
(b) A instigates B to murder D. B in pursuance of the instigation stabs D. D
