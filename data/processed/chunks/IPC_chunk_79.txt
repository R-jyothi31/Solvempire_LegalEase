Section 99. Act against which there is no right of private defence
There is no right of private defence against an act which does not reasonable 
cause the apprehension of death or of grievous hurt, if done, or attempted to 
be done, by a public servant acting in good faith under colour of his office, 
though that act, may not be strictly justifiable by law.
There is no right of private defence against an act which does not reasonable 
cause the apprehension of death or of grievous hurt, if done, or attempted to 
be done, by the direction of a public servant acting in good faith under colour 
of his office, though that direction may not be strictly justifiable by law.
There is no right of private defence in cases in which there is time to have 
recourse to the protection of the public authorities.
