Section 479. Property mark
A mark used for denoting that moveable property belongs to a particular 
person is called a property mark.
Section 480. Using a false trade mark
[Rep. by the Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958 (43 of 1958), s. 135 and Sch., (w.e.f. 25-11-1959).]
Section 481. Using a false property mark
Whoever marks any moveable property or goods or any case, package or other 
receptacle containing moveable property or goods, or uses any case, package 
or other receptacle having any mark thereon, in a manner reasonably 
calculated to cause it to be believed that the property or goods so marked, or 
any property or goods contained in any such receptacle so marked, belong to a
person to whom they do not belong, is said to use a false property mark.
