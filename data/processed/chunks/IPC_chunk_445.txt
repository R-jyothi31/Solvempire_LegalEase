(g) Z is bathing, A pours into the bath water which he knows to be boiling. 
Here A intentionally by his own bodily power causes such motion in the 
boiling water as brings that water into contact with Z, or with that water so 
situated that such contact must affect Z’s sense of feeling; A has therefore 
intentionally used force to Z; and he has done this without Z’s consent 
intending or knowing it to be likely that he may thereby cause injury, fear, or 
annoyance to Z, A has used criminal force.
(h) A incites a dog to spring upon Z, without Z; s consent. Here, if A intends to 
cause injury, fear or annoyance to Z, he uses criminal force to Z.
