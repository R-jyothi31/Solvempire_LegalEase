Section 346. Wrongful confinement in secret
Whoever wrongfully confines any person in such manner as to indicate an 
intention that the confinement of such person may not be known to any 
person interested in the person so confined, or to any public servant, or that 
the place of such confinement may not be known to or discovered by any such 
person or public servant as hereinbefore mentioned, shall be punished with 
imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to two years 
in addition to any other punishment to which he may be liable for such 
wrongful confinement.
