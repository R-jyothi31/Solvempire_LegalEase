‘And commits such act without any excuse for incurring the risk of 
causing death’
Merely causing death, by doing an act with the knowledge that is so 
imminently dangerous that it must, in all probability cause death, is not 
murder. In order that an act, done with such knowledge, should constitute 
murder, it is necessary that it should be committed without any excuse for 
incurring the risk of causing the death or bodily injury. An act, done with the 
knowledge of its consequences, is not prima facie murder, it becomes murder 
only if it can be positively affirmed that there was no excuse. The requirements
