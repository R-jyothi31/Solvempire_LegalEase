Nothing which is not intended to cause death, is an offence by reason of any 
harm which it may cause, or be intended by the doer to cause, or be known by 
the doer to be likely to cause, to any person for whose benefit it is done in good
faith, and who has given a consent, whether express or implied, to suffer that 
harm, or to take the risk of that harm
Illustration
A, a surgeon, knowing that a particular operation is likely to cause the death of
Z, who suffers under a painful complaint, but not intending to cause Z’s death,
and intending in good faith, Z’s benefit performs that operation on Z, with Z’s 
consent. A has committed no offence.
