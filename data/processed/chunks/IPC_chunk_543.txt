(f) A intentionally deceives Z into a belief that A means to repay any money 
that Z may led to him and thereby dishonestly induces Z to lend him money. A
not intending to repay it A cheats.
(g) A intentionally deceives Z into a belief that A means to deliver to Z a 
certain quantity of indigo plant which he does not intend to deliver and 
thereby dishonestly induces Z to advance money upon the faith of such 
deliver. A cheats; but if A, at the of obtaining the money, intends to deliver the 
indigo plant, and afterwards breaks his contract and does not deliver it, he 
does not cheat, but is liable only to a civil action for breach of contract.
(h) A intentionally deceives Z into a belief that A has performed A’s part of a 
contract made with Z, which he has not performed, and thereby dishonestly 
induces Z to pay money. A cheats.
