(d) A is in a house which is on fire, with Z, a child. People below hold out a 
blanket. A drops the child from the housetop, knowing it to be likely that the 
fall may kill the child, but not intending to kill the child, and intending, in 
good faith, the child’s benefit. Here, even if the child is killed by the fall, A has 
committed no offence.
Explanation
Mere pecuniary benefit is not benefit within the meaning of Sections 88, 89 
and 92.
Section 93. Communication made in good faith
No communication made in good faith is an offence by reason of any harm to 
the person to whom it is made, if it is made for the benefit of that person.
