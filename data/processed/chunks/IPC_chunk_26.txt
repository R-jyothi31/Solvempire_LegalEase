Participation in the Criminal Act
(i) To apply section 34, apart from the fact that there should be two or more 
accused, two factors must be established: (i) common intention, and (ii) 
participation of accused in the commission of an offence. If common intention
is proved but no overt act is attributed to the individual accused, section 34 
will be attracted as essentially it involves vicarious liability but if participation 
of the accused in the crime is proved and common intention is absent, section 
34 cannot be invoked; Jai Bhagwan v. State of Haryana, AIR 1999 SC 1083.
